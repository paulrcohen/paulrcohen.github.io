David Jensen and Paul R. Cohen (1997). Mulitple Comparisons in Induction Algorithms. Submitted to Machine Learning.

Abstract:

A single mechanism is responsible for three pathologies of induction algorithms: attribute selection errors, overfitting and oversearching. In each pathology, induction algorithms compare multiple items based on scores from an evaluation function and select the item that with the maximum score. We call this a multiple comparison procedure (MCP). We analyze the statistical properties of MCPs and show how failure to adjust for these properties leads to the pathologies. We also discuss approaches that can control pathological behavior, including Bonferroni adjustment, randomization testing, and cross-validation.
